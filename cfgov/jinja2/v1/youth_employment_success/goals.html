Planning how you’ll get to work

Let’s make a plan for how you’ll get to work—getting to work on time is important for keeping your job. Having a plan and a backup option can help you be prepared so you don’t get in a bind. This tool will help you figure out your best option for getting to work and how to budget for it.

Meet Mya and Frankie, they’ll help you think about your own long-term goal.

Mya plans to bike to work

Mya decided to bike to work because there’s a bikeshare near her school. Bikeshares allow you to borrow a bike from different self-service bike stations around the city. Mya heads straight to work from class—some days she has time to walk, but usually she’s in a rush and biking is faster.

Mya’s long-term goal

I want to buy an annual bikeshare membership since my school offers a special student rate and it’d be a better deal than paying per ride. The $60 annual membership works out to a little over $1 per week. In order to save money for this, I will walk to work two days a week for the next few months.

Frankie plans to take the bus

Frankie doesn’t live near public transportation so he usually gets a ride to the closest bus stop. The bus ride is about 25 minutes long so he leaves his house at 7:10am to allow enough time to get dropped off, ride the bus, and arrive at work before his 8am shift starts.

Frankie’s long-term goal

I will take the bus five days a week, but over the next year, I’d like to save up enough money to buy a car so I don’t have to rely on friends and family for rides to the bus stop. In the meantime, I will buy a $50 monthly bus pass because it’s more cost-effective than paying $2 each way for a bus ticket.

Now let’s think through your long-term goal. Figuring out your long-term goal may help you figure out the best way to get to work now while you’re working towards this goal.
