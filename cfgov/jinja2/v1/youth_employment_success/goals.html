Planning how you’ll get to work

Getting to work on time is important for keeping your job. Having a plan and a backup option is a good idea in case you get into a bind.

This tool will help you figure out your best option for getting to work, how to budget for it, and what to do if you need a backup plan.

Meet Mya and Frankie. They’ll help you think about your plan.

Mya plans to bike to work

Mya decides to bike because it will allow her to save money. Since she doesn't live near public transit or have a car, biking is her best option. She puts aside $20/month to cover bike repairs.

Mya’s long-term goal

Mya's long-term goal is to buy an electric bike and someday a car, but in the meantime, she will save money by riding her bike and will take rideshares when she gets stuck.

Frankie plans to take the bus

Frankie decides to take the bus because it conveniently drops him off near work. The bus ride is about 30 minutes long so he leaves his house at 7:15 AM to allow enough time to get there before his 8 AM shift starts.

Frankie’s long-term goal

Frankie's long-term goal is to save up for a car. He buys a $50 monthly bus pass instead of spending $2 per bus ride, allowing him to set aside the extra money he saves each month.

Thinking through your long-term transportation goal.

Figuring out your long-term goal may help you decide the best way to get to work now while you’re working towards your goal.
